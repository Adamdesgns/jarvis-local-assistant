# JARVIS — "How to Set Up JARVIS" Video Script & Shot List

A ready-to-record tutorial script. Each scene has **ON SCREEN** (what to show/click)
and **SAY** (voiceover). Read it straight through with any screen recorder
running (OBS, Xbox Game Bar `Win+G`, ShareX, etc.). Target length ~6 minutes.

Tip: record at 1080p, JARVIS maximized. Do a silent dry-run clicking through
once so the live take is smooth.

---

## 0 · Cold open (0:00–0:15)
**ON SCREEN:** JARVIS already open on the amber sphere, "LOCAL CORE ONLINE" in
the top bar. Let the sphere breathe for a beat.
**SAY:** "This is JARVIS — a private AI assistant that runs entirely on your own
Windows PC. No account, no subscription, and nothing leaves your machine unless
you choose it. In the next few minutes I'll set it up from scratch. Let's go."

## 1 · What you're looking at (0:15–0:45)
**ON SCREEN:** Point the cursor to the sphere, then the command bar at the
bottom, then the top-right buttons (＋ MODULES, EDIT LAYOUT, gear).
**SAY:** "The glowing sphere is JARVIS itself — it reacts as it listens and
thinks. Down here is the command bar: you can type, or once voice is set up,
just say 'Hey Jarvis.' Up top: Modules to show panels, Edit Layout to move them,
and the gear — that's Settings, where we'll do all the setup."

## 2 · Open Settings (0:45–0:55)
**ON SCREEN:** Click the **gear (⚙)** icon, top right. Settings opens.
**SAY:** "Click the gear to open Settings. Everything we need is on this one
screen — top to bottom."

## 3 · Free Local Voice — "Hey Jarvis" (0:55–1:45)
**ON SCREEN:** Highlight the **FREE LOCAL VOICE** card. Click **INSTALL / REPAIR
LOCAL VOICE**. Show the progress lines. (If it's a long install, cut to it
finished — status light green, "HEY JARVIS IS ACTIVE.")
**SAY:** "First, free local voice. This installs the 'Hey Jarvis' wake word and
offline speech recognition right on your laptop — no API key, no cloud. Click
Install / Repair Local Voice and let it run. When the light turns green, JARVIS
is listening for its name. If anything looks off later, the Voice Diagnostics
button walks you through it."

## 4 · The Local Brain — Ollama (1:45–2:45)
**ON SCREEN:** Highlight the **LOCAL BRAIN** card. Open the **MODEL SIZE**
dropdown (show SMALL / BALANCED / ADVANCED). Pick **BALANCED**. Click **CONNECT
/ REPAIR OLLAMA**. If Ollama isn't installed, click **INSTALL OLLAMA FOR
WINDOWS** and note the browser opens.
**SAY:** "Next, the local brain — this is what lets JARVIS actually hold a
conversation, and it runs on your own machine through a free tool called Ollama.
Pick a model size — Balanced is the sweet spot for most PCs. If you don't have
Ollama yet, hit Install Ollama for Windows, run the installer, then come back
and click Connect. One note: file search, tasks, and commands all work even
without this — Ollama just adds open-ended conversation."

## 5 · Optional cloud brain (2:45–3:30)
**ON SCREEN:** Scroll to **CLOUD BRAIN** and **CLAUDE BRAIN** cards. Click into
the **API KEY** field, type a fake key, then click the **eye icon** to reveal it
(show the value toggling). Point to **BRAIN MODE** (Local / Cloud / Auto).
**SAY:** "This part is optional. If you've got slower hardware, you can plug in
your own OpenAI or Claude key for a cloud brain — it's encrypted right here on
your PC, and you can remove it anytime. Quick tip: use the little eye icon to
double-check you pasted the key correctly. Brain Mode lets you choose local
only, cloud only, or auto — cloud with local as backup. If you're keeping it
100% private, just skip this."

## 6 · Behavior + your NEW skins (3:30–4:30)
**ON SCREEN:** Scroll to **BEHAVIOR**. Type a name in **YOUR NAME**. Toggle
**SPOKEN REPLIES** and **HEY JARVIS** on. Point to **MINIMIZE TO ORB** and
**START WITH WINDOWS**. Then open the **SKIN** dropdown and hover **CLASSIC
AMBER** / **COMMAND CENTER BLUE** (don't switch yet — tease it).
**SAY:** "Behavior settings: put in your name, turn on spoken replies and the
Hey Jarvis wake word. Minimize to Orb keeps a little floating sphere on screen,
and Start with Windows launches JARVIS when you sign in. And new here — Skins.
You can run the classic amber look, or flip the whole interface into a blue
command-center dashboard. We'll try that in a second."

## 7 · Folders JARVIS may search (4:30–5:05)
**ON SCREEN:** Scroll to **FOLDERS JARVIS MAY SEARCH** and **PROJECT
WORKSPACES**. Click **＋ ADD SEARCH FOLDER**, pick a folder. Assign a project
folder via **CHOOSE**.
**SAY:** "This is important for privacy: JARVIS only ever searches the folders
you list here — nowhere else on your computer. Add the folders you actually want
it to help with, like Documents or Downloads. And you can pin project
workspaces, so 'open the bench' jumps straight to that folder."

## 8 · Save + first command (5:05–5:35)
**ON SCREEN:** Click **SAVE SETTINGS**. Back on the main screen, click the
command bar, type `what can you do` and press EXECUTE. Show the response +
the sphere reacting.
**SAY:** "Hit Save. Now let's use it — I'll type 'what can you do,' and JARVIS
answers locally. You can also say 'Hey Jarvis' followed by your command, ask it
to find a file, or open one of your documents and ask questions about it."

## 9 · Switch to the Command Center skin (5:35–6:05)
**ON SCREEN:** Open Settings → **SKIN** → **COMMAND CENTER BLUE** → Save. The
whole UI flips to the blue dashboard. Point to the live CPU/RAM, projects,
tasks, activity. Click **MINIMIZE** on the dock to show the floating orb, then
restore.
**SAY:** "And here's the fun part — flip the Skin to Command Center Blue, and
JARVIS becomes a full movie-style dashboard: your real system stats, projects,
tasks, and activity, all live. Minimize drops it to the floating orb, same as
always. Switch back to Classic anytime — it remembers what you picked."

## 10 · Backup + outro (6:05–6:30)
**ON SCREEN:** Settings → **BACKUP & RESTORE** → hover **EXPORT MY DATA**. Close
Settings. End on the sphere.
**SAY:** "Last thing — Backup and Restore lets you export everything to a file,
and your API keys are never included. That's it: local, private, and yours.
JARVIS is a free download — link's below. Thanks for watching."

- [ ] Mic tested; room quiet.
- [ ] JARVIS maximized; Settings scrolled to top before you start.
- [ ] Do one silent dry-run, then record for real.
- [ ] Keep each click deliberate — pause a half-second so viewers can follow.
